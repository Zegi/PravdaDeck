QUICK START GUIDE

How to Play

As my future co-author, here is a quick tour of your new workplace. Let's look at the main screens you'll be interacting with.

1. The Editor's Desk

  • 1. Mailbox: Keep an eye on it. This is where poll results and special correspondence will land.
  • 2. Staff: Mechanic under construction. You can safely ignore this button for now.
  • 3. Inbox: The most important button! This is where journalists submit their drafts. Click here to start your day.
  • 4. Upgrades: Invest in your newsroom. I highly recommend purchasing the Fact-Checking perk early on.
  • 5. Exit: To leave the office.

2. Managing Articles

Here, you decide the fate of each piece:

  • Publish (+): Let it go to print as is.
  • Reject (-): Kill the story.
  • Edit: Send it to your desk for revision before publishing.

Once you've made your choices, click Next to proceed to the editing phase (or skip straight to the next day if no editing is required).

P.S.: For now, your edits don't impact your relationship with the journalists who wrote them. I plan to revisit this mechanic soon...

3. The Editing Process

Hover over the green text to alter the tone of the article. Your choice of words directly impacts the article's truthfulness, faction loyalty, readership, and voter support.

Pro Tip: A specific tone might just unlock brand-new storylines!

Don't forget to use the Fact-Checking tool (if you bought the upgrade). When you're satisfied, hit Print to publish the issue and start a new day.

P.S.: Pressing Num 1 opens debug mode, instantly revealing how the article affects loyalty... but that's cheating :-)

EDITOR'S MEMO: DEMO VERSION

  • Don't worry about money. In the demo version, you can't lose due to bankruptcy. Experiment!
  • Story photos. Pay attention to articles with photos! These are important story articles that determine how the narrative unfolds.
  • Faction secrets. Each party has 2 unique events: one at negative loyalty and one at positive. Can you collect them all?
  • You've got mail. Check your mailbox — sometimes representatives from various factions will write to you with special offers.
